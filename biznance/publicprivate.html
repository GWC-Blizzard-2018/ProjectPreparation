Public and Private Universities

Public and Private Universities: The Key Differences

Public Universities are usually funded and overseen by the State Government. This means they are usually cheaper than Private Universities. There is a big size difference between Public and Private as well. Because Private Universities are more expensive they are also usually smaller. Some Public Universities are huge. Public Universities have a large acceptance rate but are very expensive for those who are out of state. Where as state residency is less important at private universities meaning there is often a broader spectrum of people.

Private universities are often inclusive, and promote good community behavior as most rely on you living on campus. The student lifestyle experience private universities have could not be replicated by public universities generally because of how large the student base is. However, this doesn't make the experience of a public university any less than a private.

Public universities aren't typically inclusive and many student lifestyles can merge. At public universities you have the option to partake in clubs, and a lot of the time public universities have multiple campuses with lots of different lifestyles going on within them. They offer a lot of ways for students to engage and get involved in their communities.
