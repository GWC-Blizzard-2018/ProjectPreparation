Community College

Community College is a type of 2-year postsecondary education. Students often attend it in order to fulfill general education requirements, then transfer to a 4-year university. However, there are also various other benefits to attending community college, despite its reputation for weaker academics. Since many students are not directly taught about these options, here are the differences between community colleges and universities.

Community College

    Cheaper tuition

    Preparation for transfer to a 4-year college

    Schedule is more flexible

    Generally assumed that academic standards are lower, but have actually significantly improved and made these colleges very popular

University

    Has more facilities, sports, and a more active student life

    More clubs and classes

    Hard to get into some colleges

    Can be costly
